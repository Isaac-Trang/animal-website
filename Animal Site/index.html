Owl

A nocturnal bird of prey known for its large eyes, silent wings, and powerful hunting skills.

    Features of the Owl:
  1. Owls have sharp night vision and very sensitive hearing.
  2. Many owls can rotate their heads up to 270 degrees.
  3. They have soft feathers that help them fly silently.
  4. Owls use their hooked beaks to tear food into small pieces.
  5. Some owl species have excellent camouflage to blend into trees.
  6. They can spot prey from far away even in low light.
    Habitat of the Owl:
  • Owls live in forests, deserts, grasslands, and sometimes cities.
  • They often nest in trees, cliffs, or abandoned buildings.
  • Many owls prefer quiet places with plenty of shelter and food.
  • Some species live near wetlands or open fields where small animals are common.
  • They are found on every continent except Antarctica.
  • Some owls even live in snowy tundra regions where they hunt small mammals.
